Brief Biography

I am a postgraduate student who will soon graduate from the Shanghai Institute of Microsystems. My research focus is on near-field imaging in the terahertz band. I have recently published a paper in the prestigious ACS journal. My academic background is in physics, but during a visit to NUS (Singapore), I became interested in microelectronics and decided to pursue it during my postgraduate studies. My research interests cover a wide range of topics, including finite element simulation, computational electromagnetics algorithms, device fabrication and AI. I am proficient in using various industrial software and familiar with micro/nano scale devices fabricating methods. In addition to my academic pursuits, I am also an active member of the student organization at the ZTE Shanghai Branch. My responsibilities include organizing and planning group building and event activities.

I like to play Go, though I play it sloppily. I like to drink coffee, and then when I go to the on-campus cafe I will make my own coffee and drink it, and if I succeed in getting a job I can also make coffee for everyone. I also used to like to take camera out for photos, but less so now. Since I started my undergraduate degree, I feel like I've been checking out all kinds of new and interesting articles, especially on new industries and new technologies. Personality I am not willing to please others. Then I met a SSE employee. He felt that my personality is suitable for industry research, but I would like to regard myself as an investigator for the world. For future career planning, I hope to play my advantage of knowing a little bit about all aspects of the microelectronics industry, in such a windy point now to have a steady accumulation of future life. The accumulation is on the one hand the improvement of financial conditions and on the other hand the ability to continue to observe the world and gain more experience to become an expert in the industry. What I tell all my friends is that when I am old I will go back to a small place and open a coffee shop and "enjoy life".

Skills in working

When I was an undergraduate, I studied in Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics, and received basic scientific training, including reading, writing and experimental design.
Later, I went to Shanghai Institute of Microsystem and Information Technology, and started my postgraduate studies. As a student of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, I spent my first year at University of Science and Technology of China. In one year's study, I learned the manufacturing technology of optoelectronic devices, and also learned the skills of image processing. This is the most memorable moment in my life. I made many real friends and learned many skills beyond my major.
Now I am going back to Simit institute to complete my postgraduate course. I can use simulation software such as COMSOL, PSpice, the circuit design software L-Edit, Illustrator, PhotoShop and other post-processing software. I can independently simulate the electric magnetic wave behaviour though both commercial software or by self-program. And I am also familiar with the semiconductor fabrication procedures, as I received training from ShanghaiTech Quantum Device Lab.

Recommanded Books

Romance of the Three Kingdoms is such a book that shows the struggle and wars in the late Han Dynasty. Leaders of different groups have a common goal, that is, to control the whole of China. With the help of brave generals and wise advisers, they created one of the greatest romantic stories in the world. This is my favorite book, which is suitable for those who love heroism and pursue extraordinary life.

《百年梦忆·梁实秋人生自述》

Liang is an outstanding writer in China. His words have the power of humor, full of his thoughts on life. I still remember the cover he wrote for the readers. On the cover, he wrote, "I know that one of the most important behaviors of growing old is that it couldn't stop recalling past life. Now, I will do so in this biography." It's really touching to read his words and learn his attitude towards life.
